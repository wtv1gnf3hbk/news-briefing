The Pentagon revealed that [140 U.S. service members](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/world/middleeast/us-injuries-iran-war.html) have been wounded in the Iran conflict, the first official casualty count as the war enters what Defense Secretary Hegseth called the ["most intense" phase](https://www.bbc.com/news/live/cd70wzw9vqlt) yet. Iranian civilians are bearing the brunt of the escalating strikes, with many telling reporters that ["nothing will remain of Tehran"](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/world/middleeast/iran-bombing-tehran.html) if the bombing continues. Iran has responded with its own [threat of escalation](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/3/10/iran-war-live-trump-says-conflict-will-be-over-soon-40-killed-in-tehran), vowing "eye for an eye" warfare if the U.S. and Israel target critical infrastructure, Al Jazeera reports. The stakes are rising as Trump's messaging about the conflict's duration remains [all over the place](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/us/politics/trump-iran-war-how-long-timeline.html), leaving allies and markets guessing about his endgame.
