Defense Secretary Hegseth is promising today will be the ["most intense"](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/03/10/world/iran-war-trump-us-israel) day of the U.S. air campaign against Iran yet, as the conflict enters what appears to be a critical phase. The Pentagon chief says Iran is "badly losing" and warned strikes will intensify, [according to AFP](https://www.france24.com/en/video/20260310-replay-pentagon-chief-hegseth-says-iran-badly-losing-strikes-to-intensify). But Tehran is showing no signs of backing down. Iran's new supreme leader has vowed to [fight on](https://www.timesofisrael.com/iran-vows-to-fight-as-long-as-it-takes-threatens-trump-with-elimination/) and threatened Trump with "elimination," the Times of Israel reports.

The human cost is mounting on both sides. At least 40 people were killed in Tehran strikes, [Al Jazeera reports](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/3/10/iran-war-live-trump-says-conflict-will-be-over-soon-40-killed-in-tehran), with residents describing scenes of devastation. Iran has sunk at least one U.S. warship, and there are reports of Iranian sailors' bodies being repatriated after their own vessel was destroyed. The conflict has also triggered [environmental concerns](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/world/middleeast/iran-oil-strikes-health.html) as strikes on fuel depots create "black rain" across parts of Iran.

Trump's mixed messaging isn't helping clarify the endgame. He's simultaneously promised the war will end "very soon" while threatening to "go further" if Iran keeps blocking oil supplies. That uncertainty is rattling markets and sending gas prices soaring, creating [political headaches](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/09/us/politics/trump-iran-gas-prices-gop-agenda.html) for his midterm agenda.
