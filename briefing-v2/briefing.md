Good morning. Here's the state of play:

Iran's response to weeks of military pressure has been swift and economically devastating. The regime has effectively [strangled the Strait](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/12/us/politics/strait-of-hormuz-iran-war.html) of Hormuz, choking off a vital artery for global oil supplies and sending energy prices soaring despite Trump's decision to temporarily ease sanctions on Russian oil. The waterway, which handles about a fifth of the world's petroleum trade, has become the focal point of Iran's asymmetric warfare strategy as it seeks to inflict maximum economic pain on the West.

The conflict continues to spread beyond Iran's borders. Israel has [escalated attacks](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/03/13/world/iran-war-trump-oil-israel) against Hezbollah in central Beirut, while Al Jazeera reports that [Israel launched](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/3/13/iran-war-live-trump-says-war-going-well-as-gulf-under-wave-of-attacks) fresh attacks on Tehran itself. The violence has reached NATO's doorstep, with Turkey reporting that alliance defenses [shot down](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/13/world/middleeast/turkey-nato-iran-missile.html) a third Iranian missile. Even Gulf states that have tried to stay neutral are getting pulled in, as Iranian drones and missiles test regional air defenses from Saudi Arabia to Oman.
