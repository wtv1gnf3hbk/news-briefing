Good morning. Here's the state of play:

The Iran war has [escalated dramatically](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/11/iran-declares-us-israeli-economic-banking-interests-in-region-as-targets), with Iranian forces targeting commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz and launching [drone strikes](https://www.france24.com/en/dubai-airport-and-saudi-oil-field-among-targets-of-iranian-drones) near Dubai's airport and Saudi oil facilities, according to Al Jazeera. Three cargo vessels were hit by what authorities are calling "unknown projectiles," with [one ship](https://www.bbc.com/news/live/cd70wzw9vqlt) still burning in the strait that handles about a third of global oil shipments, according to the BBC. Iran has declared that U.S. and Israeli economic interests across the region are now legitimate targets.

Behind the scenes, a clearer picture is emerging of how we got here. The [Trump administration](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/us/politics/how-trump-miscalculated-iran-response.html) appears to have fundamentally misjudged Iran's willingness to escalate, betting that Tehran would seek to contain the conflict rather than expand it across the Gulf. That miscalculation has [reshaped global politics](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/11/world/europe/trump-iran-war-global-impact.html) in just a week, with energy markets in chaos and Trump now trying to [manage the war](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/11/business/economy/trump-iran-markets-economy.html) with financial markets uppermost in his mind.

**Business/Tech**

• [Gas prices](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/11/business/gas-prices-oil-iran.html) have jumped for eleven straight days, with oil pushing higher despite America being a net energy exporter. The war isn't just disrupting petroleum. Critical supplies from the region include [helium and sulfur](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/business/iran-war-impact-helium-urea-sulfur.html), creating shortages that ripple through everything from semiconductor manufacturing to fertilizer production.

• [LNG suppliers](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/shell-declares-force-majeure-on-lng-contracts-from-qatar) are declaring force majeure on contracts from Qatar as the conflict threatens energy infrastructure across the Gulf, according to Bloomberg. Japan announced it will [unilaterally release](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/japan-to-release-oil-from-reserves-unilaterally-takaichi-says) oil from strategic reserves, according to Bloomberg.

• Meta [acquired Moltbook](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/technology/meta-moltbook-social-ai-bots.html), the social network designed exclusively for AI bots. It's a telling sign of where the company thinks social media is heading when humans aren't necessarily required.

• YouTube is [rolling out](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/technology/youtube-deepfakes-detection-tool.html) new tools to help public figures report fake videos, as deepfake technology makes it easier to create convincing false content.
