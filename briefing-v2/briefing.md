Good morning. Here's the state of play:

The Middle East is spiraling into what looks like full-scale war after Israel and the US killed Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei in coordinated strikes yesterday. Iran has [launched retaliatory attacks](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/2/28/live-israel-launches-attacks-on-iran-multiple-explosions-heard-in-tehran) across the region, hitting airports in Dubai and Abu Dhabi, damaging luxury hotels in Bahrain, and targeting US bases in Iraq, according to Al Jazeera. Israel is now conducting a [second wave of strikes](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/03/01/world/iran-attack-khamenei-trump/israel-launches-new-military-strikes-as-iran-retaliates-after-khameneis-killing) on Tehran, while Iran's government has formed an interim council to manage the transition after losing its leader of nearly four decades.

Trump is doubling down on what amounts to regime change in Tehran, threatening Iran with force "never seen before" if attacks on Israel continue. The [CIA helped pinpoint](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/01/us/politics/cia-israel-ayatollah-compound.html) the gathering of Iranian leaders before Israel struck, and European allies are notably distancing themselves from the operation. Oil markets are spiking, airlines are canceling thousands of flights, and protests have erupted outside US consulates across the region. This could be Trump's defining foreign policy moment, but it's also his biggest gamble.

**Business/Tech**

- Oil prices are surging as OPEC+ members [agree in principle](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-01/opec-agrees-in-principle-to-206k-b-d-hike-for-april-delegates) to a 200,000 barrel per day production increase for April, Bloomberg reports, though that won't offset supply fears from the Iran crisis.

- Airlines across the Gulf are extending flight cancellations as Iran's strikes [damaged Dubai airport](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-01/iran-strikes-disrupt-thousands-of-flights-dubai-airport-damaged) and forced airspace closures from Kuwait to Qatar, per Bloomberg.

- Silicon Valley is [rallying behind](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/27/technology/anthropic-trump-pentagon-silicon-valley.html) Anthropic in its standoff with Trump over AI safety protocols for Pentagon contracts, setting up a test case for how artificial intelligence will be deployed in warfare.

- Wall Street is shifting to ["haven-first" strategies](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-01/wall-street-turns-to-haven-first-strategies-during-iran-attacks) as global earnings show a shift away from US markets just as the S&P 500 took a hit, according to Bloomberg.

- **Europe**: Belgium's special forces [seized a Russian "shadow fleet" tanker](https://www.scmp.com/news/world/europe/article/3345031/belgium-special-forces-seize-russian-shadow-fleet-tanker) suspected of sanctions evasion, the South China Morning Post reports, as NATO countries crack down harder on vessels helping Moscow bypass oil restrictions.

- **Asia**: At least nine people were killed when Shiite protesters [stormed the US Consulate](https://apnews.com/article/pakistan-protesters-attacked-us-consulate-karachi-iran-a3cc0c3c6f126cc54a657f61d312a203) in Karachi over Khamenei's killing, AP reports.

- **Africa**: Ghana says Russia "lured" at least 55 of its citizens to fight in Ukraine where they were killed, the [Guardian reports](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/feb/27/ghana-men-killed-fight-russia-ukraine). Rwanda-backed rebels have [entered a safe-haven city](https://www.wsj.com/articles/rwanda-backed-rebels-enter-congo-safe-haven-city-aid-hub-09fdccb8) in eastern Congo that serves as a major aid hub, the Wall Street Journal reports, escalating a conflict that's already displaced millions.
